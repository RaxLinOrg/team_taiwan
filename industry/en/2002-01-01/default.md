# Keyboard: Global Peripheral Leader

In 2002, Taiwan's dominance in the computer peripheral market extended to keyboards, where it became the world's largest producer. Combining mechanical expertise with electronics integration, Taiwanese manufacturers supplied the vast majority of keyboards bundled with global PC brands and sold at retail.

Companies like Lite-On, Chicony Electronics, and Darfon Electronics were the primary drivers behind this success. These firms excelled in developing various technologies, from traditional membrane keyboards to high-end mechanical switches and the sleek "chiclet" keys used in modern laptops.

The keyboard industry in Taiwan focused on ergonomic design, multi-language support, and the integration of multimedia functions. This specialization ensured that Taiwan remained the go-to partner for any company looking to produce reliable and innovative input hardware for the digital age.

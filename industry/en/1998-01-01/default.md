# LCD Monitor: Global Display Leadership

In 1998, Taiwan's display industry reached a turning point as it became the world's leading producer of LCD monitors. This achievement marked the beginning of a long period of dominance in the global flat-panel display market, fueled by massive investments in Thin-Film Transistor (TFT-LCD) technology.

Major players such as Acer (later separating its display division as BenQ/AUO) and Chi Mei Electronics spearheaded this expansion. These companies invested billions of dollars in "Generation" fabs, rapidly scaling production to meet the global shift from bulky CRT monitors to sleek, energy-efficient LCD screens.

The success was built on a robust ecosystem that included glass substrates, color filters, and driver ICs. By capturing the majority of the global market share, Taiwan played a pivotal role in making LCD technology affordable for consumers worldwide, cementing its position as an indispensable hub in the global electronics supply chain.

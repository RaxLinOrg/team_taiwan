# Laptop ODM: Notebook Computer Dominance

By 1999, Taiwan had achieved undisputed dominance in the design and manufacturing of notebook computers. Taiwanese Original Design Manufacturers (ODMs) became the silent force behind almost every major global PC brand, including Dell, HP, Apple, and IBM.

Companies like Quanta Computer, Compal Electronics, and Wistron developed a unique business model that went beyond simple manufacturing. They took over the entire design and engineering process, allowing global brands to focus on marketing and sales. Quanta Computer, for instance, rose to become the world's largest notebook manufacturer during this period.

The efficiency of Taiwan's "983" logistics model (delivering 98% of orders within 3 days) ensured that Taiwanese ODMs could manage the rapid product cycles of the tech industry. This era solidified Taiwan's role as the primary architect and builder of the world's portable computing devices, a position it still largely maintains today.

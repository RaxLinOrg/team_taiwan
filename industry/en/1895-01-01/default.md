# Camphor Kingdom

In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, Taiwan was known as the "Camphor Kingdom." At its peak, Taiwan produced over 70% of the world's camphor, which was an essential ingredient for celluloid (an early plastic) and smokeless gunpowder.

The camphor industry was a significant driver of Taiwan's early modern economy and led to the development of infrastructure and forestry management across the island.

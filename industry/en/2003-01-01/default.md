# Network Equipment ODM: Connecting the World

As the internet age exploded in the early 2000s, Taiwan became the world's factory for network equipment. By 2003, Taiwanese manufacturers produced the majority of the world's DSL modems, routers, and Wireless Local Area Network (WLAN) devices.

Major players like D-Link, Zyxel, and Gemtek, along with ODMs like Hon Hai (Foxconn) and Accton Technology, played a vital role in the global rollout of broadband internet. They worked closely with global telecommunications companies to design and manufacture the hardware that brought high-speed connectivity into homes and offices worldwide.

Taiwan's strength lay in its ability to rapidly integrate new wireless standards (like Wi-Fi) into cost-effective consumer products. This leadership in networking hardware provided the physical infrastructure for the modern connected world, from early home networks to the complex digital ecosystems we use today.

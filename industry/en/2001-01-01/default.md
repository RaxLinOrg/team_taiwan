# Mouse: Global Peripheral Leader

By 2001, Taiwan had captured over 60% of the global market for computer mice. This achievement highlighted Taiwan's ability to dominate high-volume consumer electronics peripherals through precision manufacturing and design innovation.

Logitech, though founded in Switzerland, built its manufacturing and engineering backbone in Taiwan (Hsinchu Science Park). Meanwhile, home-grown companies like Primax Electronics and Chicory (and later peripheral divisions of major ODMs) specialized in delivering high-quality, reliable input devices for the world's leading PC brands.

Taiwanese firms led the transition from mechanical ball mice to optical and laser technologies. By optimizing the integration of optical sensors and wireless communication, Taiwan ensured that this essential interface tool became more precise, durable, and affordable for users around the globe.

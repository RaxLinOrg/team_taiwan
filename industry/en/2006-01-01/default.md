# Bicycle Components: High-end Supply Chain

In 2006, while Taiwan was famous for its technology, it also reached a pinnacle in the traditional manufacturing sector by dominating the high-end bicycle component market. Taiwan became the critical link in the global supply chain for premium cycling equipment.

Companies like Giant Manufacturing and Merida Industry moved beyond just making frames to fostering a whole ecosystem of component specialists. Firms like KMC (the world's largest chain manufacturer), Tektro (brakes), and various specialized manufacturers for hubs, rims, and transmissions made Taiwan the go-to source for quality bicycle parts.

This success was driven by the "A-Team"—an alliance of Taiwanese bicycle manufacturers who collaborated to improve production quality and efficiency. By focusing on carbon fiber technology and precision engineering, Taiwan transformed itself from a mass-producer of cheap bikes into the global hub for high-performance cycling innovation.

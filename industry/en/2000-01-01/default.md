# Motherboard: The Foundation of Global PCs

In 2000, Taiwan reinforced its position as the world's motherboard production hub, accounting for nearly 75% of global output. As the "heart" of the personal computer, the motherboard required complex integration of chipsets, circuits, and components—a field where Taiwanese engineering excelled.

Leading firms like ASUS, Gigabyte, MSI, and ASRock became household names among PC enthusiasts and industry professionals alike. These companies didn't just manufacture; they innovated in areas like thermal management, overclocking, and power efficiency, setting the standards for the entire PC industry.

The concentration of motherboard production in Taiwan fostered a dense network of component suppliers, from capacitors to connectors. This ecosystem allowed for rapid prototyping and mass production, ensuring that whenever a new CPU was released by Intel or AMD, Taiwanese motherboards were ready to support it on day one.
